WHAT IS INSIDE "GROSS PROFIT"
✓ The gross margin % IS covering
COGS · manufacturing · raw materials · inbound freight · packaging · shipping to customer · payment gateway fees · returns & refunds · warehousing · marketplace fees
Gross profit = Revenue × Margin % − Total ad spend
✗ NOT included (fixed costs)
Agency fees · tech stack (Shopify, Klaviyo) · office rent & salaries · founder salary · brand & creative production · corporate taxes · interest & finance charges
Fixed costs are paid regardless of ad spend, so they don't belong in the "is the next ₹ of ad spend worth it?" decision. That's why gross profit (not net profit) is the right number here.

Model limits & assumptions 9 things this calculator doesn't model
1. This tool models paid digital ads only — not total business revenue
Revenue shown here is the revenue attributed to paid digital channels (Google, Meta, and similar). It is NOT your total business revenue. If your total business is much larger than your online paid ads spend, the curve you see is a slice of the business, not the whole picture. When presenting, be explicit: "this is what happens to the paid digital revenue, which is a portion of total revenue."
2. Halo effect is real and uncounted
Paid ads lift organic search, direct traffic, email performance, branded search, and for many brands, offline store visits. This calculator credits zero of that halo. The real ROI of paid ads is usually 15–30% higher than what you see here (sometimes far more for strong brands). The tool is intentionally conservative — if anything, the case for scaling is stronger than the numbers suggest.
3. Awareness revenue is underestimated on purpose
Default Awareness ROAS is 0x because brand-building pays back through channels that are hard to measure directly: branded search lift, offline sales, view-through conversions, LTV lift. Real contribution is positive but hard to quantify. If you want to focus purely on performance channels, set the awareness spend to zero.
4. Fixed costs and awareness spend should be proportional, not total
If your company's total fixed costs are large but digital is only a small slice of total business revenue, don't subtract the whole company's overhead from digital ad profit. The "Proportional allocation" toggle lets you scale both fixed costs and awareness spend to digital's share of the business. Set the digital % higher than the raw share if you believe digital awareness has an outsized effect on digital channels specifically.
5. The profit peak may not be reachable in 1–2 months
Even if the math says "profit peak is at 5× current spend," you cannot scale overnight. Learning phase re-triggers, CPC inflation, creative fatigue, operational bottlenecks, and cash-flow gaps all limit practical scale-up to roughly 20–30% per month. The profit peak is the theoretical maximum, not next month's budget.
6. Elasticity changes with seasonality, creative, and competitors
This model uses one fixed elasticity number. In reality, elasticity rises during peak season and with fresh creative, and falls in off-season or when competitors scale aggressively. Re-run this tool every month with the latest data — don't treat one run as a permanent answer.
7. Gross margin can shift as you scale
Aggressive scaling often pushes the product mix toward lower-margin items (discounts, entry-level SKUs, clearance). Real blended gross margin may drop 2–5 percentage points at much higher spend levels. If you're planning a 3× scale, it's worth modelling a scenario with a slightly lower margin to stress-test the conclusion.
8. Attribution noise grows with scale
At small spend, ROAS signals are clean. At 5× spend, cross-device journeys, view-through conversions, and platform-reported conversions add more noise. Don't trust last-click ROAS blindly at higher spend levels — cross-check with back-end/GA4 numbers, and be conservative about "reported" ROAS gains from scaled campaigns.
9. This is a model, not a forecast
The math is directional. It exists to illustrate the shape of the response curve for your specific inputs — not to commit to a specific monthly revenue number. Use it as a discussion framework, not as a hard forecast or KPI target.
Scope: Models paid digital ads only (Google, Meta, and similar performance and awareness channels). Does not include organic, direct, email, marketplace, or offline revenue — see the cautions section above for what this tool does and doesn't capture.

Model assumptions: Performance revenue follows a diminishing-returns curve with elasticity ≈ 0.76 (+10% spend → +7.5% revenue, +20% spend → +15% revenue, +30% spend → +22.5% revenue). Awareness contribution grows as √spend (heavier diminishing returns, because awareness takes longer to convert and has a 60–90 day real-world lag). Gross profit = Revenue × Margin % − Total spend.

This is a model, not a forecast. It exists to make the shape of the argument visible. Plug in your own numbers above to stress-test the story against your account.
